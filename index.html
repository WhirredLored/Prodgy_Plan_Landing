Why we're building this

The options pet parents are given at the worst moment of their week are broken.

When your dog eats something they shouldn't, or your cat stops acting like herself, you're handed a short list of bad choices. Pay thousands you don't have. Sign up for a credit card you don't understand. Trust an insurance policy you read about on a billboard. Or make a decision no pet parent should have to make.

  • 1

    Pet insurance is still insurance — monthly premiums, deductibles, exclusions, and a company that profits when you don't use it. Works for some people. Doesn't work for most.

  • 2

    Medical credit cards are deferred-interest products. Miss the promotional window and you owe six months of retroactive interest at rates approaching 27%. Your vet hates handing them out.

  • 3

    Paying cash at the moment of crisis is the default for everyone who can't qualify for the above — and it's why so many pets don't get the care their families would absolutely provide if the system gave them a path.

What we're doing about it

Care built around the relationship between a pet parent and their vet.

The right unit of trust in veterinary care isn't a third-party insurer. It's the clinic that already knows your pet. Our platform gives independent practices the infrastructure to offer affordable, predictable care plans directly — with financing that looks at your behavior, not your credit score.

No credit checks for financing

Eligibility is earned through how you show up — consistent payments, care follow-through, honest communication. Every pet parent has a path.

Clear rules, no hidden penalties

If you talk to us, we work with you. Our system is designed so that silence escalates and honesty resolves. The math is transparent and written down.

Your clinic stays your clinic

We don't compete with your vet. We give them the tools to offer you something better than a handshake and a credit card application. The relationship you already have with your practice is the point.

Not insurance. Not a lender. Not a middleman.

A care participation program, built in Iowa, starting with the clinics and families who've been asking for something like this for a long time.
